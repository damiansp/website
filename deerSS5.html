Fetal Sex Ratios

Here we model the proportion of males to females among the fetuses. This can be thought of as a binomial distribution, where there is a certain probability of any fetus being a male. Hence, we model this as a GLM with a binomially distributed response, with the (default) logit link function.

sr.glm.full = glm(cbind(maleNo, femaleNo) ~ 
                  (age + intensity + I(intensity^2) + yearF + cwd)^2 - 
                  intensity:I(intensity^2) + section,
                  family = binomial, data = deerF1)
sr.glm = step(sr.glm.full, direction = 'both', trace = 0)

se = function(p, n) {
    return (sqrt(p * (1 - p) / n))
}

seTot = se(propMTot, (nMale + nFemale))

The graph shows the proportion of male fetuses each year ± 1.96 SE (95% confidence interval).

Looking just at the numbers of male and female fetuses, we see that over our entire data set, males are slightly more prevalent than females (51.4% or a M:F ration of 1.06), though this value just misses significance at the 95% confidence level. Looking at each year individually, no year differed significantly from the 50% proportion, nor did any year differ significantly from any other year.
