Professional Experience

  • Owner and Principal. Phillips Research and Analytics. Eugene, OR. 2014 - Present.
  • Lead Scientist, Data Analyst, and Webmaster. Northwest Arctic Borough; Planning Dept. Kotzebue, Alaska. 2013 - 2014.
    The Northwest Arctic Borough is comprised of several native Iñupiaq communities. I was hired on as the lead scientist for a mapping project. Via interviews with elders and experienced hunters and gatherers, we developed a GIS database of geographical regions that are important for subsistence and food security in this region, as well as documenting local knowledge about subsistence species' ecology. The Borough (a native government body) intends to use these data to help direct future planning in the region in such a way that food security may be maintained. I was responsible for developing and administering the methods, analyzing the data, a shared in the GIS responsibilities. A public release of these data is anticipated in for early 2016. Samples of the final product may be made available upon request. Working drafts of the project methods can be found here or here (academic paper), and here (in-house methods document).
    I also manage the Borough's website.
  • Postdoctoral Research Associate. Illinois Natural History Survey (Prairie Research Institute, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign). 2011 - 2013.
    Statistician and mathematical modeler for research in conservation biology including various types of regression/linear modeling, PCA, hill-climbing optimization algorithms, agent-based models (simulations), analysis of variance (ANOVA), etc. Research focused on wildlife epidemiology, population dynamics, reproduction, food-webs and quantitative fatty acid signature analysis.
