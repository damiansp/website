Principal Components Analysis Visualization

This graphic shows the results of a principal components analysis (PCA) on 60 fatty acids from fat samples from each of 228 animals, including river otters, and a number of their prey species: various fish, crayfish, amphibians, and molluscs.

As the output demonstates, different taxonomic groups show distinct fatty acid profile "signatures", forming distinct clusters in the PCA plot.

In the section on using PCA to determine species from fat samples, I mention that one of the difficulties lies in visualization of high-dimensional data. While a lot of the information can be consdensed and displayed visually using just the first two or three principal components (PCs), often higher PCs will also contain information of interest to the researcher. Here I have taken the PCA scores output in the R script, and used them as input for this interactive visualization that allows the user to

display the data projected onto any of the top 10 PCs in the data set.

For simplicity, I have omiited PCs above 10, and color-coded the points according to these taxonomic divisions: mollusc, crayfish, fish, frog, otter (tail fat and footpad fat displayed in diffferent colors--see the PCA link above for details).

Key: Mollusc Crayfish Fish Frog Otter (footpad) Otter(tail)
